BSc Engineering Honours in Mechanical Engineering

A four-year, full-time programme designed to develop technically competent, ethically grounded, and globally competitive mechanical engineers.

The undergraduate programme provides a solid grounding in the fundamental sciences — mathematics, physics, thermodynamics, mechanics, and materials — before progressing into advanced engineering design, manufacturing, fluid mechanics, robotics, and sustainable energy systems.

Students undertake laboratory work, design projects, an industrial training placement, and a final-year research project, ensuring they graduate with both theoretical depth and practical experience.

Elective Specialisations

🤖

Mechatronics & Robotics

Autonomous systems, sensors, actuators, PLC programming and robotic design.

🌡️

Energy Systems

Advanced thermodynamics, renewable energy, HVAC, and combustion engineering.

🏭

Mechanical Systems

Machine design, dynamics, fluid dynamics, FEA and automobile engineering.

💼

Technopreneurship

Business, finance, marketing, and entrepreneurship for engineers.

Entry Requirements

✓

Sri Lanka Advanced Level examination with passes in Combined Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry.

✓

Admission through the University Grants Commission (UGC) centralised system based on Z-score ranking.

✓

Minimum English language proficiency required — students are expected to study through the medium of English.

Postgraduate Programmes

The Department offers advanced research-based and coursework postgraduate degrees, nurturing the next generation of engineering researchers, academics, and industry leaders.

Postgraduate students work closely with experienced academic supervisors, have access to well-equipped research laboratories, and are encouraged to publish in internationally recognised journals and conferences. Collaboration with industry and overseas universities is actively facilitated.

Degree Programmes

Master of Science

MSc in Mechanical Engineering

A coursework-based master's programme offering advanced study in core and elective mechanical engineering modules, culminating in a research dissertation.

Duration1.5 – 2 Years
ModeFull-time / Part-time
EntryBSc Eng (Hons) ≥ 2nd Class
Intake20 students / year

Master of Philosophy

MPhil in Mechanical Engineering

A full research degree focused on original investigation in a chosen area of mechanical engineering, supervised by a senior academic staff member.

Duration2 – 3 Years
ModeFull-time
EntryBSc Eng (Hons) ≥ 2nd Class Upper
IntakeRolling admissions

Doctor of Philosophy

PhD in Mechanical Engineering

The highest academic qualification offered by the Department, requiring a substantial original contribution to knowledge in mechanical engineering or a related field.

Duration3 – 5 Years
ModeFull-time
EntryMPhil or MSc (Research)
IntakeRolling admissions

Professional Development

Postgraduate Diploma in Engineering

A flexible professional development programme for practising engineers, offering specialist coursework modules without a full research component.

Duration1 Year
ModePart-time
EntryBSc Eng + 2 yrs experience
Intake25 students / year

Entry Requirements

✓

A recognised Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering or a closely related engineering discipline, with at least Second Class standing.

✓

For PhD admission, a relevant MPhil or research-based Master's degree is normally required.

✓

English language proficiency — IELTS 6.0 or equivalent for international applicants.

✓

A research proposal outlining intended area of study is required for MPhil and PhD applications.

✓

Two academic or professional references are required as part of the application.
