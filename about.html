About the Department

Who We Are

The Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Peradeniya is one of the oldest and most distinguished engineering departments in Sri Lanka, committed to excellence in education, research, and service to society.

Established within the Faculty of Engineering, the Department offers a comprehensive undergraduate programme leading to the Bachelor of Science and Engineering Honours in Mechanical Engineering, alongside a range of postgraduate programmes at Masters and Doctoral levels.

Our academic staff comprises highly qualified engineers and researchers with expertise spanning a broad spectrum of mechanical engineering disciplines — from thermodynamics and fluid mechanics to robotics, manufacturing technology, and sustainable energy systems. The Department maintains state-of-the-art research laboratories and actively collaborates with industry, government agencies, and international institutions.

We pride ourselves on producing graduates who are technically proficient, analytically sharp, and ethically grounded — engineers who are ready to lead in a rapidly evolving world. Our alumni hold prominent positions in industry, academia, and public service across Sri Lanka and internationally.

Our History

From humble beginnings in the post-independence era to a nationally recognised centre of engineering excellence — the journey of the Department of Mechanical Engineering spans over seven decades.

The Department traces its origins to the founding of the Faculty of Engineering at the University of Ceylon, Peradeniya in the early 1950s. Since then, it has grown steadily in academic strength, research output, and international recognition.

1950

Foundation of the Faculty of Engineering

The Faculty of Engineering is established at the University of Ceylon, Peradeniya. Mechanical Engineering is among the founding disciplines, offering a general engineering programme.

1966

Department of Mechanical Engineering Established

The Department gains independent status within the Faculty. A dedicated four-year undergraduate degree programme in Mechanical Engineering is introduced.

1978

First Postgraduate Programme Launched

The Department introduces its first MPhil programme, attracting students from across South Asia. Research activities expand significantly with new laboratory facilities.

1995

IESL Accreditation Achieved

The undergraduate programme receives full accreditation from the Institution of Engineers, Sri Lanka (IESL), recognising the quality and rigour of the curriculum.

2008

Research Centres Established

Specialised research centres in Energy Technology, Manufacturing Systems, and Robotics are established, catalysing collaborative industry–university research projects.

2018

International Collaborations Expanded

MoUs signed with universities in Germany, Japan, and Australia, enabling staff and student exchange, joint research publications, and shared laboratory resources.

2024

New Engineering Complex Inaugurated

A state-of-the-art engineering complex housing modern laboratories, a design studio, and a postgraduate research hub is officially inaugurated, marking a new chapter for the Department.

Programme Objectives

The undergraduate programme in Mechanical Engineering is designed to produce graduates who meet the highest standards of the engineering profession.

The Programme Educational Objectives (PEOs) describe the career and professional accomplishments that the programme is preparing graduates to achieve within a few years of graduation.

01

Apply fundamental knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering to analyse, design and develop solutions to mechanical engineering problems in industry and beyond.

02

Demonstrate proficiency in the use of modern engineering tools, software, and experimental techniques relevant to the mechanical engineering discipline.

03

Function effectively as individuals and as members or leaders of diverse, multi-disciplinary teams engaged in engineering practice or research.

04

Communicate engineering concepts, results, and proposals clearly and effectively in both written and oral forms to technical and non-technical audiences.

05

Recognise ethical responsibilities and the impact of engineering decisions on society, the economy, and the environment, and act accordingly with professional integrity.

06

Engage in lifelong learning and professional development to remain current with advances in technology, research, and engineering practice throughout their careers.

07

Contribute to the national development of Sri Lanka through innovative engineering solutions that address real-world challenges in energy, manufacturing, infrastructure, and sustainability.
